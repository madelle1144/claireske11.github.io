About Us

Claire's Community Centre is a project founded in 2008 as a Community Based Organization (C.B.O) working in Dandora slums in Nairobi. Claire's implements child centred programmes basically in education, feeding and health services to the school going aged children.

Challenges & Strategy

Problem Statement

Dandora slums face numerous challenges including poverty, poor sanitation, and limited access to quality education. Many children drop out of school due to lack of school fees, hunger, or the need to work and support their families.

The community struggles with high unemployment rates, drug abuse, and teenage pregnancies, which further exacerbate the challenges faced by children in accessing education.

Our Strategy

Our holistic approach addresses education, nutrition, and health to break the cycle of poverty. We provide a safe learning environment, nutritious meals, and basic healthcare to ensure children can focus on their education.

We engage parents and the community through awareness programs and income-generating activities to create a supportive environment for children's education.

Statement of Need

ECDE Program

Our Early Childhood Development Education program provides a strong foundation for children aged 3-6 years, preparing them for primary education through play-based learning.

Primary Education

We offer quality primary education following the Kenyan curriculum, with small class sizes and individualized attention to ensure each child reaches their potential.

Junior Secondary

Our junior secondary program prepares students for national exams and equips them with life skills for their transition to high school or vocational training.

Our educational programs are designed to meet the unique needs of children from disadvantaged backgrounds, providing not just academic instruction but also psychosocial support, mentorship, and career guidance to help them break the cycle of poverty.
